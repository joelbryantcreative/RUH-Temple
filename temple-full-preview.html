01 — Purpose

Fragrance became something
we wear without feeling.

Across ancient cultures — Egypt, Arabia, Persia — scent was never casual. It marked ceremony, love, mourning, and prayer. It was the invisible layer between the human and the sacred.

Somewhere along the way, it became a marketing decision. A bottle on a shelf. A logo on skin.

RŪḤ exists to reverse that shift.

"This brand is not just fragrance.
It is a feeling you wear."

To return fragrance
to a ritual

Lift the lid.
Meet the poem first.

II

Unwrap.
Slowly.

Untie the jute cord. Unfold the papyrus. The concrete bottle is revealed — rough, warm, heavy. An object that asks to be held before it is used.

III

Wear it like
a prayer.

Apply the oil as a deliberate act. Not a finishing touch. A foundation. The scent was not made — it was remembered. Now it belongs to you.
